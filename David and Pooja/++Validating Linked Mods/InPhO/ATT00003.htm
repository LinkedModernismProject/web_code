I did the alignment into Wikipedia and Colin did the confidence ratings today. We think that things in the 8-9 confidence range are highly reliable, but the things with a 7 or lower need manual review. Things at this level may match just a last name or a first name, but not both. That leaves you with 1784 good alignments, and about 659 to go through and review. Things with a 3 or lower are basically a lost cause.

We'd recommend having a someone go through and add another column and just mark Y if it is a match and N if it isn't for a hand spot-check. After that, you could either look for good matches for the unmatched ones, or just move on with what you've got.

There's two files, a raw "tsv" (tab-separated values) file in Unicode (UTF-8), which is great for a programmer to mess with. I went ahead and made a native Excel version just to keep all the accent marks for manual review if using a spreadsheet program. Either way can be transformed back to something programmable.
